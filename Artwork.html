Less is m​o​re

As Albert Einstein once said, "Everything should be made as simple as possible, but not simpler." In my poster designed to promote the school's TOK exhibition, I aimed to convey the idea of "less is more," suggesting that knowledge should be concise and easily understandable. 

This design choice creates a striking contrast between the vibrant background and the solid-colored text area, drawing the audience's attention and highlighting the theme of the exhibition.

Fading, then dying

This black-and-white piece uses simple lines and strong contrasts to illustrate life's fragility and strength. 

The geometric shapes on the right symbolize fragmentation, while the net-like structures on the left convey collection

creating a contrast between flow and tension, hinting at the relationship between birth and uncertainty.

Foreseeing the future

He sat quietly in the office, flipping through childhood memorabilia, once a skateboarding prodigy now forced to abandon that passion. Gazing out at the vibrant skyscrapers and flickering billboards, unease washed over him—he could be replaced at any moment.

This cold, ruthless city devoured dreams. Daily pressures shadowed him, and his once-proud skills faded into black-and-white remnants. Each late night at work marked a silent farewell to his aspirations. Fear grew, knowing one day he might find himself sharing the streets with stray dogs, forgotten and adrift. In this harsh reality, he grasped each moment tightly, yet felt powerless to change his fate.

Etching the marks of time with poetry, eternal and moving.

He was a banished poet, living in the shadow of the eastern fence, yet a vibrant garden bloomed in his heart. Every night, as the city lights flickered to life, he emerged from darkness with his pen and paper, sketching the orchids of his imagination. His verses dripped like morning dew, casting a mysterious glow across the city.

Once overlooked, the orchids began to bloom, their awakening stirred by his recitations, as if magic ignited deep emotions within the people. When someone paused to listen, color revived in their eyes, and the gray city breathed with new life.

People whispered that this poet could weave dreams with reality, restoring color to the monochrome streets. Though a solitary figure, his poetry flowed like a warm breeze, painting every soul with vibrant hues.
